⚠️ Severe Thunderstorm Alert for Ontario! ⚠️

Date: June 13th, 2024

Time: Afternoon/Evening

A significant severe weather event is expected to impact Ontario, with threats including tornadoes, wind gusts over 100 km/h, large hail, and heavy rainfall. Stay informed and take necessary precautions!

There is a threat of significant tornadoes along the shores of Lake Huron stretching across Barrie, Orillia, and through Cottage Country. Individuals in these locations today should be alert and ready to take action should severe weather approach. Stay alert to all Environment Canada Alerts.

Threat Levels:

  • Level 4: Bracebridge, Orillia, Barrie, Newmarket
  • Level 3: Hanover, Owen Sound, Kincardine, Guelph, Toronto
  • Level 2: London, Belleville, Smiths Falls
  • Level 1: Windsor, Chatham, Brockville

Specific Threats:

  • Tornado: 1 or 2 possible
  • Wind Gusts: 125+ km/h
  • Hail: 3-4 cm
  • Flooding: 50-75 mm

Areas of Highest Concern: Orillia, Bracebridge, and surrounding regions.

Safety Tips:

  • Secure outdoor objects.
  • Stay indoors and avoid windows during storms.
  • Prepare an emergency kit.
  • Monitor local weather updates and alerts.
